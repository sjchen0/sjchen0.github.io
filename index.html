I am a second-year Ph.D. student at Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Princeton University. Previously, I obtained B.Sc. in Computer Science with First Class Honours from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, with a minor in Mathematics.

My research interest lies broadly in optimization, statistics, and their association with the theoretical foundations of modern machine learning and financial decision making. Recently, I am in particular focused on the decision theory behind language model decoding strategies.

My hobbies include piano, soccer (football), photography, among others.
